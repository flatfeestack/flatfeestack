Flatfeestack is a flat-fee sponsoring platform.

Flatfeestack distributes funds based on git metrics. Let's face it, without open source software, your software project would not exist.

Our Mission

Open source contributors should be able to make a living with open source software

Dream

Some individuals already live their dream of developing open source software and manage to make a decent living. However, those are exceptions rather than the norm.


Support

For a flat fee of $32 per quarter (or $125 per year), you can support as many open source projects as you like. Your developer badge shows which projects you support.


Money

Earn money if your open source project is backed. Your project badge shows the sponsors. To receive funding, your open source project needs a public git repository.

Why Flatfeestack?

Donation services such as Open Collective, Patreon, or Github Sponsors support project or person: e.g., support project X with $50 per year, or support person Y with $50 per year. This is a good start, but the more projects or people you support, the more you pay. When coming across an interesting or promising project, we often end up not supporting, as we would have to support many other interesting projects. With flatfeestack, you pay a flat-fee, never more.

How does it work?

Each project receives your donation equally divided. Simply donate and don't worry about how many projects you want to support, it is always $32 per quarter (or $125 per year). It is a similar concept to Flattr for open source projects, where git metrics decide how much each contributor earns

Can an organization donate as well?

Flatfeestack is compatible with your accounting department. Sponsoring open source projects is difficult for a company. Try to convince your accounting department to sponsor. Flatfeestack is compatible with your accounting department. If the accounting department calculates $1500/per year per developer for its IT cost, with Flatfeestack it is $125 more: $1625. And the developer can support as many projects as you want. In addition the organization gets an up-to-date list of supported open source projects.

Why Cryptocurrencies?

While the donation payment is done with credit cards, the payout is with cryptocurrency. We chose Binance Coin, as the energy footprint is much lower compared to Ethereum and Bitcoin. With crypto currencies, the payout is without restrictions and anyone can receive funding. While Github is finally available in Iran, it took them two years to get the license . These restrictions do not apply for cryptocurrencies. For selecting crypto currencies, we have the following priorities:

1) energy-friendly,
2) popularity,
3) decentralization.
